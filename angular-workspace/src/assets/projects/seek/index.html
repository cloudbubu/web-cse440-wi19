SEEK is Bringing Sexual Education into the 21st Century

Supplement learning in your traditional classroom environment and engage discreetly with content tailored to your needs.

Sexual Education

Fact: Only 13 US states currently require that sexual education courses contain scientific and medically accurate information. The reality of this statistic, coupled with the fact that many students often look to their peers or the internet for guidance, can lead to unsafe practices and the spread of misinformation. Our goal is to improve the quality of sexual education by enabling access to verifiable information and resources.

Empowerment

Around adolescence is when students begin to develop their identities and explore new ideas. Sexual education courses provide information necessary to aid in this pursuit; however, they also contain sensitive topics that may be difficult to discuss in a classroom setting. Providing students with a comfortable environment in which they can have these conversations and grow a sense of confidence is a priority of ours.

Knowledge

Our research has shown that students are unsatisfied with the breadth of material covered in generic sexual education classes. Valuable topics such as Consent and Healthy Relationships are often excluded from curriculums, and nearly all sexual education courses neglect to address information specific to gender and sexual minorities. We aim to solve this by delivering students content relevant to their unique identities and interests.

Features

The SEEK application has four main features.

Home Page Assignments List

The Home Page displays a list of assignments and their corresponding due dates. Students can click on any assignment to be brought to the video for that assignment. Once checkpoints in an assignment are completed, progress is highlighted under the assignment title in the Home Page.

Video Assignments using Customizable AI Agent

This is where an AI agent will lead students through a particular topic, utilizing the ability to learn about the student’s preferences and pulling from current, reliable sources to keep the conversation engaging. Students can change the look of the AI agent, as well as its relationship type (teacher, doctor, parent or friend).

Anonymous Question Tool

Clicking on the Ask a Question link in the footer navigates to the Anonymous Question Page. Here, students can send a question to their teacher anonymously. Each submitted question becomes a thread in a chat-like window, where students can view responses and submit follow-up questions later on.

Resource Center

Students can access the Resouce Center by clicking the link in the footer. The Resource Center is filled with links to reliable websites, which are segmented by relevant sex ed topics. If students want to ask their teacher a question about something they read on one of these websites, they can link the website via the ‘?’ button next to the applicable link.
